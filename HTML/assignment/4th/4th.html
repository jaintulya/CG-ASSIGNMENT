History of Computers and the Internet

Early Computers

The abacus was the first simple calculator. Charles Babbage designed the Analytical Engine, and Ada Lovelace wrote the first algorithm. Early machines were mechanical and slow. Later, electromechanical devices used electricity. The CPU became the brain of the computer. The WWW was still far in the future.

Generation of computer

First generation: vacuum tubes, large and hot. Second generation: transistors, faster and smaller. Third generation: integrated circuits, powerful chips. Fourth generation: microprocessors, personal computers. Fifth generation: AI, robotics, and quantum computing. Old punch cards were deleted, new cloud systems inserted. Formula: E = mc2. Water is H2O. Example: x2 + y2 = z2.

Birth of the Internet

The Internet started with ARPANET, a U.S. research project. It connected universities and allowed online communication. HTML made websites possible. The Internet was not built in a day Bill Gates said:

The Internet is becoming the town square for the global village of tomorrow.

Modern era

The WWW grew in the 1990s.Companies like Google, Microsoft, and Apple changed the world. Smartphones, social media, and AI now define the digital revolution. Cybersecurity is strong, ethics are important. IoT connects smart homes and cities. The Internet is now everywhere.
